# LeaseFlow — 2:55 Demo Video Script

## 00:00–00:25 — A real workflow bottleneck

I built LeaseFlow because my husband was trapped in a serious leasing workflow bottleneck. I use AI every day, but he does not. So instead of asking him to become an AI power user, I used Codex to turn the work he already does into one calm, shared workspace.

## 00:25–00:50 — Source files become structured updates

Landlords send new leasing facts in files: available floors, areas, parking, incentives, dates, and floor plans. A team member selects the building and source type, uploads the original file, and LeaseFlow converts it into structured proposed changes while preserving the source.

## 00:50–01:15 — Human review controls publication

AI never publishes operational facts by itself. The data steward reviews every extracted change. A senior then approves the new version. Only published, active, authorized information becomes current, and an older floor plan is blocked from future customer packages.

## 01:15–01:40 — One current record, on web and mobile

Once approved, the same current record appears everywhere. The web view shows every leasing field and a direct link to the original source file. The mobile app uses that published record in the field, so the whole team works from the same answer without learning a new AI interface.

## 01:40–02:05 — Requests become approved packages

A call or email request becomes a structured task. LeaseFlow confirms the request, selects only current shareable materials, prepares the message and recipients, and still requires a human to review and approve before delivery. The demo records the send without pretending to integrate with a production mailbox.

## 02:05–02:30 — Weekly reporting starts with the landlord

Weekly reporting is organized by landlord, because one landlord meeting can cover several buildings. The team configures the buildings, meeting schedule, required sections, owners, approvers, and recipient group once. LeaseFlow then gathers the week’s approved building activity into reports that remain building specific and human approved.

## 02:30–02:55 — Codex for Everyone

Codex helped me move from conversations, real workflow corrections, and messy source examples to a tested product prototype. LeaseFlow is my first example of Codex for Everyone: AI adapts to the worker, instead of asking every worker to become an AI expert.

All property data, organizations, contacts, and source files shown in this demo are synthetic.
